## Context of All Three AI Mentions

### Mention 1 (approx. p. 20): Infrastructure for AI
> "innviði, til að stuðla að nýsköpun og gera atvinnulífi kleift að **nýta gervigreind til aukinnar verðmætasköpunar**."

Translation: "...infrastructure, to promote innovation and enable businesses to **utilize AI for increased value creation**."

**Framing:** AI as a tool for business value creation. No downside mentioned.

### Mention 2 (approx. p. 22): Skills for AI
> "Tryggja þarf færni til að **nýta gervigreind** á íslenskum vinnumarkaði á ábyrgan hátt til að auka framleiðni vinnuafls."

Translation: "Skills must be ensured to **utilize AI** in the Icelandic labor market responsibly to increase labor productivity."

**Framing:** AI skills as a productivity driver. "Ábyrgan hátt" (responsible way) is the closest the document gets to acknowledging any risk, but it refers to responsible USE, not to employment risk.

### Mention 3 (approx. p. 29): Data Centers
> "Alþjóðleg eftirspurn eftir þjónustu gagnavera eykst hratt, einkum vegna aukinnar notkunar **gervigreindar**. Ísland er í sterkri stöðu til að raungera tækifæri í gagnaversiðnaði í ljósi m.a. samkeppnishæfni íslenskrar raforku og hagfelldra veðuraðstæðna fyrir kælingu."

Translation: "International demand for data center services is growing rapidly, particularly due to increased use of **AI**. Iceland is in a strong position to realize opportunities in the data center industry given the competitiveness of Icelandic electricity and favorable weather conditions for cooling."

**Framing:** AI as CAUSE of demand for data centers. Iceland positioned to profit from AI infrastructure demand.

## Analysis

The document's relationship to AI can be summarized in one sentence:

**AI appears exclusively as something Iceland should profit from (data centers) and utilize (productivity), never as something that could displace workers.**

The complete absence of:
- "atvinnuleysi" (unemployment) 
- "sjálfvirknivæðing" (automation)
- "endurmenntun" (retraining)
- "röskun" (disruption)

...in a document titled "Employment Policy" that covers the period to 2035 is a structural gap that cannot be explained by editorial choice. These are foundational concepts in any employment policy that acknowledges 21st-century economic reality.
