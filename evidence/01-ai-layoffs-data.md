## Company-Specific Data

### IBM
- **Scale:** ~7,800-8,000 back-office/HR roles paused/eliminated
- **AI System:** AskHR chatbot handles 94% of employee questions
- **CEO Statement:** Arvind Krishna (May 2023): "I could easily see 30% of workforce getting replaced by AI"
- **Nuance:** Actual HR cuts were ~200 initially; total workforce has gone UP (rehired in other areas)
- **Krishna follow-up:** "IBM hired more programmers and salespeople" after AI cuts
- **Sources:**
  - [Bloomberg, May 2023](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2023-05-01/ibm-to-pause-hiring-for-back-office-jobs-that-ai-could-kill)

### Klarna

- **Quote:** "Cost was a predominant evaluation factor" leading to "lower quality"
- **Sources:**

## Disconfirming Evidence

1. **IBM's total workforce went UP** despite AI cuts in HR - they rehired in programming and sales
2. **Klarna reversed course** - the pure AI replacement model failed on quality
3. **Marc Andreessen** (March 2026): "Companies are 75% overstaffed and AI is the 'silver bullet excuse' to clean house" - suggesting AI is cover for existing overstaffing
4. **Sam Altman:** "There's some AI washing where people are blaming AI for layoffs that they would otherwise do"
5. **NBER CFO Survey:** Despite 9x growth, projected AI displacement is only 0.4% of US workforce
6. **Challenger data shows overall layoffs DOWN:** Q1 2026 total (217,362) is down 56% from Q1 2025
7. **Duolingo:** No full-time employees laid off despite "AI-first" branding
8. **Many layoffs are "restructuring" relabeled:** Companies may cite AI for investor appeal
