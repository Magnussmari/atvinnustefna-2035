# Sector 3: Institutional Reports on AI and Employment

**Research Date:** 2026-04-10

## World Economic Forum - Future of Jobs Report 2025

- **Published:** January 2025
- **Key finding:** 22% of jobs will be disrupted by 2030
- **Jobs created:** 170 million new roles
- **Jobs displaced:** 92 million
- **Net change:** +78 million
- **AI specifically:** AI creates 11M jobs, displaces 9M
- **Employer plans:** 41% of employers plan to reduce workforce due to AI
- **Skills transformation:** 39% of existing skills will be transformed/outdated by 2030
- **Worker transition:** Nearly half of employers expect to transition exposed staff to other roles
- **URL:** [WEF Future of Jobs 2025](https://www.weforum.org/publications/the-future-of-jobs-report-2025/)
- **PDF:** [Full report](https://reports.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Future_of_Jobs_Report_2025.pdf)

## IMF - AI and the Global Economy (January 2024)

- **Key finding:** ~40% of global employment exposed to AI
- **Advanced economies:** ~60% of jobs exposed
- **Of exposed jobs:** Half may benefit (productivity); half face displacement
- **Emerging markets:** 40% exposure; low-income countries: 26%
- **Inequality warning:** AI could worsen inequality between and within nations
- **Georgieva (2026):** Called AI "a tsunami hitting the labour market"
- **URL:** [IMF Blog, January 2024](https://www.imf.org/en/blogs/articles/2024/01/14/ai-will-transform-the-global-economy-lets-make-sure-it-benefits-humanity)
- **Full report:** [IMF SDN/2024/001](https://www.imf.org/-/media/files/publications/sdn/2024/english/sdnea2024001.pdf)
- **CNBC coverage:** [CNBC, January 2024](https://www.cnbc.com/2024/01/15/imf-warns-ai-to-hit-almost-40percent-of-global-employment-worsen-inequality.html)

## Goldman Sachs - Generative AI Economic Impact (March 2023)

- **Key finding:** 300 million full-time jobs exposed globally to automation
- **Scope:** 18% of work globally automatable (employment-weighted)
- **GDP impact:** Could increase global GDP by 7% over 10 years
- **Most affected countries:** Hong Kong, Israel, Japan, Sweden, US
- **Least affected:** China, Nigeria, Vietnam, Kenya, India
- **Most affected jobs:** Office/administrative, law, architecture, engineering
- **Adoption timeline:** ~10 years for widespread adoption (base case)
- **Displacement rate:** 6-7% of workers displaced over transition decade
- **Unemployment impact:** +0.6 percentage points over decade
- **URL:** [Goldman Sachs](https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/articles/generative-ai-could-raise-global-gdp-by-7-percent)
- **CNBC coverage:** [CNBC, March 2023](https://www.cnbc.com/2023/03/28/ai-automation-could-impact-300-million-jobs-heres-which-ones.html)

## McKinsey Global Institute - Economic Potential of Generative AI (June 2023)

- **Total value:** $6.1-7.9 trillion annually
- **Use case value:** $2.6-4.4 trillion across 63 use cases
- **Automation potential:** 60-70% of employee work activities could be automated
- **Timeline:** 50% of work activities automated between 2030-2060 (midpoint 2045)
- **Key sectors:** Customer operations, marketing/sales, software engineering, R&D (75% of value)
- **US productivity:** +0.5-0.9 percentage points annually through 2030
- **URL:** [McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/tech-and-ai/our-insights/the-economic-potential-of-generative-ai-the-next-productivity-frontier)

## OECD Employment Outlook 2025

- **Key finding:** 27% of OECD employment in occupations at highest automation risk
- **Training gap:** Only 1/3 of workers aged 60-65 participated in training (2023) vs. half of 25-44
- **Policy recommendation:** Age-inclusive hiring, job-search assistance, wage insurance, continuous skill development
- **Regulatory gap:** "many loopholes and gaps in existing regulation that leave workers vulnerable"
- **URL:** [OECD Employment Outlook 2025](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-employment-outlook-2025_194a947b-en.html)

## ILO - Generative AI and Jobs (May 2025)

- **Key finding:** 25% of global employment in occupations exposed to GenAI
- **High-income countries:** 34% exposure
- **Gender gap:** 9.6% of female employment at highest automation risk vs 3.5% of male
- **Most exposed:** Clerical jobs
- **Recommendation:** Social dialogue, social protection, skills investment, inclusive labor markets
- **URL:** [ILO, May 2025](https://www.ilo.org/publications/generative-ai-and-jobs-2025-update)

## Disconfirming Evidence

1. **WEF NET positive:** 78 million more jobs created than destroyed globally
2. **Goldman Sachs GDP growth:** 7% GDP increase could offset displacement
3. **McKinsey timeline:** Full automation midpoint at 2045 - longer than feared
4. **OECD framing:** Focuses on demographic crunch as bigger challenge than AI displacement
5. **IMF acknowledges:** Half of exposed jobs may BENEFIT from AI, not just lose
