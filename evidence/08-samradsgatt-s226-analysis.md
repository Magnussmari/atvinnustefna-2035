## Context: Two Consultations, One Silence

The Panopticon research project documented that Iceland's **AI Action Plan** consultation (S-223/2024, Mál 3862) received 12 submissions with zero from major labor unions. This finding is independently verifiable at: https://island.is/samradsgatt/mal/3862

A separate consultation was conducted for the **Atvinnustefna Íslands** itself (S-226/2025, Mál 4111). This received 94 submissions — including from ASÍ, BSRB, VR, Efling, and BHM. The question is: **what did these unions say about AI-driven workforce displacement?**

The answer: **Nothing.**

### 5. Almannarómur + CADIA + Gervigreindasetur HÍ (#49, 5 pages)
**File:** `49_Almannaromur_CADIA.pdf`
**API URL:** https://samradapi.island.is/api/Documents/1320915b-0cd2-f011-9bd3-005056bcce7e
**Signatories:** Óttar Kolbeinsson Proppé (Almannarómur), Stefán Ólafsson (CADIA), Hafsteinn Einarsson (Gervigreindasetur HÍ)

**Topics covered:**
- AI development and Icelandic language technology
- Adaptation to Icelandic conditions (language, culture, data)
- Skills development and university AI education
- Applied research and innovation
- Digital infrastructure (compute, HPC)
- AI for societal benefit

**AI/displacement analysis:**
- AI extensively discussed — but **entirely as opportunity and infrastructure**
- One acknowledgment of broader impact: "ekkert bendir til þess að áhrif gervigreindarbyltingarinnar séu fyllilega komin í ljós enn og líklegt að gífurlegar breytingar séu að vænta á ýmsum atvinnugreinum" [nothing suggests AI revolution impacts are fully visible yet; massive changes are expected across industries]
- **No displacement risk analysis, no labor market modeling, no call for measurement infrastructure**
- No reference to the ICT trifecta, the knowledge-occupation contraction, or any Hagstofa labor data
- No reference to Nordic AI governance frameworks (Hilfr2, LO-NHO, etc.)
- Focused on: language models, data collection, university programs, compute infrastructure

**Assessment:** Iceland's own AI research community — CADIA and Gervigreindasetur HÍ — submitted to the employment strategy consultation and framed AI exclusively as an opportunity requiring investment. They acknowledged "massive changes" are coming but did not call for any mechanism to monitor, measure, or mitigate the labor market consequences of those changes. The organizations best positioned to advise on AI's workforce impact chose not to.

## Synthesis: The Double Silence

| Consultation | Date | Submissions | Union submissions | AI displacement mentioned by unions |
|-------------|------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------------------------|
| **S-223/2024** (AI Action Plan) | Nov 7-21, 2024 | 12 | 1 (BHM, general) | **No** |
| **S-226/2025** (Atvinnustefna) | Nov 14 – Dec 9, 2025 | 94 | 4 (ASÍ+BSRB, BHM, VR, Efling) | **No** |

The institutional silence is not a single-consultation anomaly. It is a **pattern across both consultations**:

1. When the government asked about AI specifically (S-223), unions did not show up
2. When the government asked about employment strategy broadly (S-226), unions showed up but did not mention AI
3. When Iceland's AI research centers submitted to the employment consultation (S-226, #49), they framed AI as pure opportunity

**No Icelandic institution — labor, academic, or governmental — has produced a submission to either consultation that analyzes AI's labor market displacement risk using Icelandic data.**
