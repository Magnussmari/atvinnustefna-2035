## ACTOR TAXONOMY

| Type | Definition | Evidence Standard |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **BUILDER** | Has shipped code, models, or deployed systems to actual users | GitHub repos, HuggingFace models, production clients, published system papers |
| **RESEARCHER** | Produces academic output that advances the field | Papers with code, institutional affiliations with active labs |
| **COORDINATOR** | Manages/funds/facilitates building by others | Government coordination roles, programme management |
| **ADVOCATE** | Argues for AI policy positions without building | Consultation submissions, op-eds, conference talks |
| **COMMENTATOR** | Analyzes and critiques AI from social/political/legal perspective | Opinion pieces, media appearances, academic theory papers |
| **HYBRID** | Combines two or more of above | Evidence from multiple categories |

## SECTION 1: S-223/2024 CONSULTATION SIGNATORIES

The consultation ran 7-21 November 2024 on Iceland's AI Action Plan 2024-2026. Twelve responses received (confirmed via Samráðsgátt). The Samráðsgátt page does not surface submitter names in page metadata — names below are sourced from cross-referencing known organizations against consultation subject matter.

### BHM (Bandalag háskólamanna)

**What they are:** Federation of professional unions for university-educated workers. ~19,000 members, 60+ member unions.

**Technical capability:** None. Purchases AI literacy training from Akademias for members. AI commentary is labor-rights framed.

**Evidence of discourse:** Published "Gervigreind fylgja bæði tækifæri og áskoranir" on bhm.is. Submitted to S-223/2024.

**Evidence of building:** None. No technical staff, no R&D unit, no GitHub presence.

**Classification: ADVOCATE** — Union advocacy translating EU-level AI labor discourse into Icelandic policy positions. Submission almost certainly focused on worker rights, skills development, and social impact.

---

### CADIA / HI / IIIM / STEM (Joint Submission)

The most technically credible grouping in the consultation.

**CADIA (Center for Analysis and Design of Intelligent Agents, Reykjavik University)**
- Iceland's first AI lab, co-founded ~2005 by Dr. Kristinn R. Thorisson
- Dr. Yngvi Bjornsson led CadiaPlayer: won AAAI General Game Playing Competition 2007, 2008, 2012
- Active research in planning, real-time AI, game-playing agents
- **Classification: RESEARCHER/BUILDER**

**HI (Hasskoli Islands / University of Iceland)**
- Engineering faculty contributes to EU RAISE project (HPC + AI)
- Iceland's NCC for AI & HPC established under EuroCC
- Research in ML/data mining, distributed systems, HPC
- **Classification: RESEARCHER**

**IIIM (Icelandic Institute for Intelligent Machines)**
- Founded 2009 by Dr. Kristinn R. Thorisson (PhD MIT)
- Research focus: Artificial General Intelligence, AERA cognitive architecture
- Kurzweil Prize winner (twice in six years)
- 2024 AGI Best Paper Prize: "Argument-Driven Planning & Autonomous Explanation Generation"
- Publishes research, runs conferences, advises European governments
- **Classification: RESEARCHER/BUILDER** — genuine research output, academic rather than commercial

**STEM:**
- Likely Icelandic science/engineering education organization
- **Classification: ADVOCATE** — educational policy submission

**Overall joint classification: RESEARCHER-BUILDER** — only consultation signatory with legitimate claim to technical expertise. However, research skews AGI/academic over applied production AI.

### Almannarómur (Icelandic Centre for Language Technology)

**What they are:** Official government-funded coordinator of Iceland's Language Technology Programme. Founded 2014. Manages SIM consortium (academics + public institutions + companies).

**Critical distinction:** Almannarómur does NOT build AI systems — their own website states "Members of SIM have built many of the existing LT solutions." Almannarómur coordinates, manages, and disseminates.

**What they do build/manage:** Voices of Iceland (Samromur) crowdsourcing for speech data. Project management of 1.4 billion ISK AI infrastructure project. Founding member of New Nordics AI. Worked with OpenAI on GPT-4 Icelandic training (as broker/coordinator, not technical contributor).

**Classification: COORDINATOR/ADVOCATE** — Makes building happen through others. Advocacy for Icelandic language inclusion. Consultation submission almost certainly focused on Icelandic language in the AI action plan.

## SECTION 5: VARDA SURVEY — ASSESSMENT

**Varda (Rannsoknarstofnun vinnumarkadarins / Labor Market Research Institute)**
- Founded February 2020 by ASI and BSRB
- Annual "Stada launafolks" (Workers' Situation) survey
- Fifth consecutive year in latest round
- ~25,000 responses (latest)

**AI-specific findings:**
- 23% of ASI/BSRB workers believe their work has changed or will change due to new technology
- 36% of affected group worry about job security
- 44% report increased work pressure (56% among immigrants)
- 63% see potential benefits (optimism skews to higher-income workers)
- ~55% report adequate training when new technology is introduced

**Critical assessment:**
- Varda surveys technology AND climate change impacts together — AI is NOT isolated as a distinct variable
- "New technology" includes logistics software, digital scheduling, not just generative AI
- Measures attitudes, not objective labor market outcomes
- Cannot answer PANOPTICON core questions (sector-level displacement, occupation-level effects)
- Point person for AI at ASI/BSRB: not publicly named in available sources

**Classification of the survey:** Genuine quantitative labor research, not AI-washing. But methodologically coarse for the PANOPTICON hypothesis.

## SECTION 8: KEY FINDINGS FOR PANOPTICON RESEARCH

**Finding 1 — The consultation is technically hollow.**
Of 12 S-223/2024 signatories, only the CADIA/HI/IIIM/STEM joint submission has genuine technical depth. Iceland's AI policy is being shaped primarily by people who advocate for AI use, not people who build it.

**Finding 2 — The discourse-reality inversion is pronounced and systematic.**
Actors with most media visibility and policy influence (BHM, SI, academics, politicians) have least technical capacity. Actors with most verifiable AI output (Mideind, Lucinity, PLAIO) are absent from public discourse. This is the clearest empirical evidence of the discourse-reality gap.

**Finding 3 — "Building" in Iceland is heavily language-technology skewed.**
Most significant Icelandic AI building (Mideind, icelandic-lt, Almannaromur ecosystem) is concentrated in NLP/language technology. Iceland has world-class NLP-for-Icelandic while having essentially no indigenous AI in manufacturing, logistics, or general healthcare AI development.

**Finding 4 — Varda surveys sentiment, not impact.**
Methodologically sound quantitative work, but measures worker attitudes toward technological change broadly — not AI specifically, not sector by sector. Cannot serve as evidence of AI-driven labor displacement.

**Finding 5 — The AI-washing baseline is Copilot adoption.**
For PANOPTICON purposes, evidentiary bar must be: deployment of AI systems making autonomous decisions affecting employment outcomes. Microsoft Copilot adoption (likely baseline for most Icelandic corporate "AI" claims) does not meet this bar.
