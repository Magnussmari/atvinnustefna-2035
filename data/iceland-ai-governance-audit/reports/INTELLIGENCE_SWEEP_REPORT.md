## PART VII: SYNTHESIS — WHAT CHANGED

### 18. Five Things That Changed the Picture

**1. The Trifecta is complete.** Employment DOWN + Revenue UP + Vacancies COLLAPSED in ICT. This upgrades the evidence from "suggestive correlation" to "strong circumstantial evidence of structural displacement." No competing explanation (cyclical, offshoring, demographic) fits all three signals simultaneously.

**2. Worker experience data exists.** The Varda 2026 report (n=25,000) provides the first quantitative measure of technology's impact on Icelandic workers. The 36% fear-of-job-loss and 45% undertrained figures are citable ground truth.

**3. The institutional vacuum is worse than estimated.** Phase 1 found no union AI publications. Phase 5 found no union consultation submissions, no AI survey criteria, no congress resolutions. The silence is not passive — it is active non-engagement with a policy process that directly invited participation.

**4. The academic landscape has shifted.** The null consensus from Phase 1 (anchored by Humlum & Vestergaard) is now contested by sector-specific and age-stratified findings. The PANOPTICON ICT finding is consistent with the emerging academic view that aggregate nulls mask sectoral displacement.

**5. The government contradicted itself.** It used AI to replace administrative work (PM office, Jan 2025) while simultaneously positioning AI as "augmentation not replacement" in the Action Plan (July 2025). This is a documented inconsistency.

### 19. What Did NOT Change

- **The verdict remains QUALIFIED.** Link 2 (job loss -> welfare entry) is still missing.
- **Denmark null result** still anchors the counter-argument.
- **The 400x scale mismatch** (macro signal 9,300 vs firm-level evidence ~22) is reduced but not eliminated. Add Advania's company-wide Copilot and CCP's AI tool, the firm-level evidence grows but remains orders of magnitude below the macro signal.
- **Welfare trends are still secular.** Psychiatric diagnoses have been rising since 1990. AI is at most an accelerant, not an originator.
