## 2. DIAGNOSIS TRENDS

### Historical Arc (1990-2024)

- **1990-2007** (Thorlacius et al. 2010): Mental/behavioural disorders among NEW disability recipients rose from 14-20% to 30-35% — a doubling over 17 years
- **2000-2019** (Laeknabladid 2021): Psychiatric disorders went from ~35% to >50% of recipients. 3x more people in rehabilitation for psychiatric illness in 2016-2019 vs. 2000-2003
- **2000-2024** (Laeknabladid 2026): The fastest-growing diagnosis cluster showed **179% relative increase** vs. 26% for other categories

### Fastest-Growing Diagnosis Cluster (179% growth, 2000-2024)

- Anxiety and depression
- Soft tissue injuries
- Neurodevelopmental disorders (ADHD, autism)
- Obesity
- Pain, fatigue, stress and burnout
- Post-viral fatigue syndrome
- COVID-19 sequelae

### ADHD/Autism Signal

Iceland has ADHD diagnosis rates 2-3x comparable populations. Neurodevelopmental disorders growing fastest among 18-49 year-olds, especially women. Whether this reflects genuine need, diagnostic expansion, or pathway effects is unclear.

---

## 3. FALSIFICATION TEST RESULTS

### FB-1: Are psychiatric diagnoses rising faster than other categories?

**RESULT: CONFIRMED.** The 179% vs 26% differential is unambiguous. Psychiatric and stress-adjacent diagnoses are the dominant growth driver. This is consistent with (but not specific to) AI-related labour stress.

### FB-2: Is growth explained by aging (60-67) rather than working-age inflows (30-55)?

**RESULT: PARTIALLY FALSIFIED.** Younger cohort (18-49) growth rate (+57%) substantially exceeds older cohort (50-66, +27%). The demographic aging explanation is weakened. Something beyond pre-retirement drift is driving the younger cohort increase — though whether this is AI-related, post-pandemic, or diagnostic expansion cannot be determined.

### FB-3: Does September 2025 reform create a measurement discontinuity?

**RESULT: CONFIRMED DISCONTINUITY.** The reform:
- Eliminated disability grant category (all transferred to permanent disability pension — inflates headcount mechanically)
- Renamed rehabilitation pension to "medical and rehabilitation allowance"
- Changed eligibility assessment methodology
- Post-September 2025 data is NOT directly comparable to pre-reform data

---

## 4. SEPTEMBER 2025 REFORM DETAILS

11 major changes implemented simultaneously:
1. New disability pension category (replaces three old categories)
2. Partial disability pension introduced
3. Rehabilitation pension renamed
4. Integrated expert assessment replaces fragmented evaluations
5. Income-reduction rate standardised at 45%
6. Employment incentives strengthened
7. Extended rehabilitation timeframes
8. Assistance for persons awaiting treatment (new)
9. Job-search support upon rehabilitation completion
10. National coordination teams established
11. Service portal for provider coordination

TR is conducting research into why women 50+ are disproportionately represented — governmental acknowledgement the concentration is anomalous.

## 6. GAPS

1. **No annual time series 2019-2023** for disability/rehabilitation recipient counts in accessible form
2. **No new-entrant flow data** (only stock counts) — flow data would reveal if acceleration is recent
3. **No occupation-level data** for claimants — the critical missing link for AI displacement
4. **No sick leave pipeline data** from Sjukratryggingar
5. **Laeknabladid 2026 article** cited through secondary sources; full text not accessible
6. **COVID-19 effects inseparable** from trend data in available sources

---

## 7. INTERIM CONCLUSION

**The welfare expansion is real, large, and accelerating in the younger working-age cohort. It is driven by psychiatric and stress-adjacent diagnoses. However, the evidence does NOT confirm AI displacement as a cause.**

The data is CONSISTENT with AI displacement (psychiatric dominance, younger cohort acceleration, burnout/anxiety cluster) but EQUALLY consistent with:
- 35-year secular diagnostic trend continuing
- Post-pandemic mental health effects (COVID sequelae explicitly in the growth cluster)
- Neurodevelopmental diagnosis expansion (ADHD/autism in younger cohorts)
- Reduced stigma lowering diagnostic threshold (Thorlacius et al. 2010 conclusion)
- Structural features of the Icelandic welfare system (women 50+ in physically demanding work)

**AI displacement remains a candidate hypothesis requiring occupation-specific and flow-timing data to confirm, not an established finding.**
